# Fee Avoidance Strategies

## Strategy 1: The Right Card

Many airline-branded credit cards waive the first checked bag fee per person. Annual fee often pays for itself in 2-3 trips.

| Card | Annual Fee | Bag Savings | Best For |
|------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| United Explorer | $95 | $70/trip (2 bags) | United frequent flyers |
| Delta SkyMiles Gold Amex | $150 | $70/trip | Delta frequent flyers |
| Citi / AAdvantage Platinum | $99 | $35/trip (1 bag) | American flyers |

**Vietnam-specific**: VN Airlines Lotusmiles Visa often includes priority check-in and lounge access.

## Strategy 2: The Loyalty Game

Airlines reward loyalty with fee waivers. Even low-tier status eliminates most annoying fees.

| Status Level | Typical Benefits |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Silver/Basic | Free seat selection, priority boarding |
| Gold/Mid | +1 free checked bag, lounge access (some) |
| Platinum/Top | +2 free bags, free upgrades, lounge |

**Status match trick**: Some airlines match competitor status. Apply through their website with proof of competitor status.

## Strategy 3: The Packing Strategy

| If You Can... | You Save... | How |
|---------------|-------------|-----|
| Pack in personal item only | $35-65 per flight | Use compression cubes, wear heaviest items |
| Stay under 7kg carry-on | $0-15 (already included) | Weigh at home, not at gate |
| Ship luggage ahead | Variable | LuggageForward, SendMyBag (book 7+ days ahead) |

## Strategy 4: The Booking Channel

| Channel | Typical Extra Cost | When It's Worth It |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Airline website | Baseline | Always check first |
| Airline app | Sometimes -5% | Mobile-only deals |
| OTA (Expedia, etc.) | +$0-15 booking fee | Only if significantly cheaper |
| Travel agent | +$15-50 | Complex itineraries, corporate |
| Airport counter | +20-50% on bags | Never for bags, only last resort |

## Strategy 5: The Timing Play

- **Change fees**: Book refundable when schedule is uncertain, switch to cheaper fare once confirmed
- **24-hour rule**: In US, airlines must allow free cancellation within 24 hours of booking
- **Price drop**: Some airlines (Alaska, JetBlue) issue credit for price drops after booking
- **Fare lock**: Some carriers offer to "lock" a price for 24-48 hours for a small fee ($5-15)
