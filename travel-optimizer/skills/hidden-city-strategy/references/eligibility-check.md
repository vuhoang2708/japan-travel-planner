# Hidden City Eligibility Checklist

## Pre-Analysis Verification

ALL conditions must be met before hidden city analysis proceeds. If ANY condition fails, the analysis stops and suggests alternatives.

## Mandatory Conditions (Must ALL Pass)

### 1. Baggage Type
- **PASS**: Carry-on / personal item only
- **FAIL**: Checked baggage of any kind
- **Reason**: Checked bags are tagged to final ticketed destination. They will NOT stop at the layover city. You arrive without luggage.
- **Exception**: None. Even if you "ask nicely" at check-in, bag tags follow the ticket.

### 2. Frequent Flyer Program
- **PASS**: No FF number attached to booking (book without loyalty account)
- **FAIL**: Booking linked to any frequent flyer program
- **Reason**: Airlines track boarding patterns through FF accounts. Repeated hidden city patterns trigger automatic account review.
- **Workaround**: Book without FF number. But this means NO miles earned.

### 3. Corporate Travel Card
- **PASS**: Personal payment method (personal credit/debit card)
- **FAIL**: Corporate travel card, company expense account, or TMC (Travel Management Company) booking
- **Reason**: Corporate accounts get more scrutiny. Company travel policies usually prohibit the practice. IT/Finance can see booking vs. actual travel mismatch.

### 4. Trip Type
- **PASS**: One-way booking only
- **FAIL**: Round-trip or multi-city booking
- **Reason**: Not boarding any segment of a multi-segment booking cancels ALL subsequent segments. If you skip leg 2 of 4, legs 3 and 4 are automatically cancelled.

### 5. Corporate Policy
- **PASS**: No employer travel policy prohibiting the practice (or self-employed)
- **FAIL**: Company travel policy exists with anti-skiplagging clause
- **Reason**: Policy violation can result in disciplinary action regardless of airline response.
- **Check**: Ask Travel Manager or review corporate travel policy document.

### 6. Downstream Connections
- **PASS**: No connecting flights booked after hidden city arrival
- **FAIL**: Have connecting flights that depend on arriving at hidden city destination
- **Reason**: If the hidden city flight is delayed/cancelled/rerouted, you're rebooked to the TICKETED final destination, not your actual one.

### 7. Usage Frequency
- **PASS**: One-time or very rare use (< 2x per year on same route)
- **WARN**: Repeated use on same route (2-4x per year) - increased detection risk
- **FAIL**: Systematic regular use (weekly/monthly) - near-certain detection
- **Reason**: Airlines flag patterns. Same name + same route + always missing final segment = flagged.

## Additional Risk Factors (Don't Fail, But Increase Risk)

| Factor | Risk Impact | Note |
|--------|-------------|------|
| International route | +HIGH | Immigration may question why no onward ticket |
| Business/First class | +HIGH | Higher revenue impact = more scrutiny |
| Peak travel dates | +MEDIUM | More staff attention at busy times |
| Same airline repeatedly | +HIGH | Account-level tracking across bookings |
| Using OTA (not airline direct) | +LOW | OTAs may have anti-skiplag policies too |
| Group booking | +MEDIUM | Multiple no-shows on same PNR draws attention |

## When to Suggest Alternatives Instead

If eligibility fails, recommend:
1. **Route optimization** - legitimate hub-based savings
2. **Date optimization** - cheaper dates with similar savings potential
3. **Virtual interlining** - separate tickets that are fully legitimate
4. **Deals verification** - promo codes that achieve similar discounts
