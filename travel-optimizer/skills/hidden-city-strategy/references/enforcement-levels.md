# Airline Enforcement Levels for Hidden City Ticketing

## Enforcement Classification

Based on reported cases, airline policies, and industry trends as of 2025-2026.

## US Carriers

### United Airlines - HIGH 🔴
- **Detection**: AI-based monitoring system launched 2025
- **Known actions**: Fare difference charging, account suspension
- **Focus routes**: Major hub connections (ORD, EWR, SFO, IAH)
- **Pattern detection**: Tracks repeat skiplaggers by name, payment method
- **Likelihood of action**: High on hub routes, lower on spoke-to-spoke

### Delta Air Lines - MEDIUM-HIGH 🟠
- **Detection**: Dedicated task force at ATL hub
- **Known actions**: Account review, loyalty tier downgrade
- **Focus routes**: Atlanta connections (ATL is world's busiest)
- **Pattern detection**: Manual review of flagged itineraries
- **Likelihood of action**: Moderate, increases with frequency

### American Airlines - MEDIUM 🟡
- **Detection**: Linked to AAdvantage program monitoring
- **Known actions**: Miles forfeiture, account warning
- **Focus routes**: DFW and MIA hub connections
- **Pattern detection**: AAdvantage account tracking
- **Likelihood of action**: Moderate for loyalty members, low for non-members

### Southwest Airlines - LOW 🟢
- **Detection**: Minimal systematic detection
- **Known actions**: Rare individual cases
- **Notes**: Point-to-point model makes hidden city less common/useful
- **Likelihood of action**: Very low

### JetBlue - LOW 🟢
- **Detection**: No known dedicated system
- **Likelihood of action**: Very low

---

## Asian Carriers

### Vietnam Airlines - LOW-MEDIUM 🟡
- **Detection**: Manual review, not systematic
- **Known actions**: Individual case-by-case
- **Notes**: Less common practice in Vietnamese market
- **Likelihood of action**: Low for casual use

### VietJet Air - LOW 🟢
- **Detection**: Minimal enforcement resources
- **Notes**: LCC model - pricing already point-to-point
- **Likelihood of action**: Very low (but also less opportunity for savings)

### Singapore Airlines - MEDIUM 🟡
- **Detection**: Integrated with KrisFlyer program
- **Known actions**: KrisFlyer account review
- **Likelihood of action**: Moderate for frequent flyers

### Korean Air - MEDIUM 🟡
- **Detection**: SkyTeam alliance sharing
- **Known actions**: SKYPASS account warnings
- **Likelihood of action**: Moderate

### AirAsia / Scoot / LCCs - LOW 🟢
- **Detection**: Minimal (point-to-point pricing model)
- **Notes**: Hidden city rarely beneficial with LCCs
- **Likelihood of action**: Very low

---

## European Carriers

### Lufthansa Group - HIGH 🔴
- **Detection**: Advanced systematic monitoring
- **Known actions**: Has sued passengers for fare differences
- **Legal precedent**: Won court case in Germany requiring passenger to pay €2,112 difference
- **Focus**: Business class hidden city (highest revenue impact)
- **Likelihood of action**: High, especially for premium cabins

### Ryanair - LOW 🟢
- **Detection**: Minimal (already point-to-point pricing)
- **Likelihood of action**: Very low

### Turkish Airlines - LOW-MEDIUM 🟡
- **Detection**: Not systematic
- **Likelihood of action**: Low

---

## Middle East Carriers

### Emirates - MEDIUM 🟡
- **Detection**: Integrated with Skywards program
- **Likelihood of action**: Moderate for loyalty members

### Qatar Airways - MEDIUM 🟡
- **Detection**: Privilege Club monitoring
- **Likelihood of action**: Moderate

---

## Key Trends (2025-2026)

1. **AI Detection Increasing**: United leading with automated flagging
2. **Alliance Sharing**: Airlines share data within alliances (Star Alliance, SkyTeam, oneworld)
3. **Legal Precedent**: Lufthansa's successful lawsuit emboldens other carriers
4. **LCCs Unaffected**: Point-to-point pricing makes hidden city largely irrelevant
5. **Pattern > Individual**: Airlines focus on repeat offenders, not one-time incidents
6. **Payment Tracking**: Same credit card used repeatedly on hidden city routes triggers flags
