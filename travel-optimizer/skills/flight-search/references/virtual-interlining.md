# Virtual Interlining Guide

## What is Virtual Interlining?

Virtual interlining means combining flights from different airlines into a single itinerary - even though these airlines have no formal partnership (no codeshare, no interline agreement). The traveler buys separate tickets and self-connects.

## Why It Saves Money

Airlines price based on O&D (Origin & Destination) demand. A popular route (e.g., HAN→SFO) commands premium pricing. But breaking it into two legs on different carriers can exploit:
- LCC pricing for one leg (VietJet HAN→ICN: $120)
- Legacy carrier pricing for the other (United ICN→SFO: $400)
- Total: $520 vs $1,100 for VN Airlines HAN→SFO direct

## Self-Connection Rules

### Minimum Connection Time (MCT) - Self-Transfer

| Scenario | Minimum Time | Recommended |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Domestic → Domestic | 2 hours | 3 hours |
| Domestic → International | 3 hours | 4 hours |
| International → International | 3 hours | 4+ hours |
| With checked bags (recheck) | 4 hours | 5+ hours |

### Critical Checklist for Virtual Interlining

1. ✅ Separate tickets purchased - understand no delay protection
2. ✅ Sufficient layover time (see MCT above)
3. ✅ Carry-on only preferred (avoid bag recheck complexity)
4. ✅ Check visa/transit requirements at hub country
5. ✅ Allow buffer for immigration/customs if international → international
6. ✅ Consider travel insurance covering missed connections
7. ✅ Download both airlines' apps for real-time flight updates
8. ❌ Do NOT check bags through - they won't transfer
9. ❌ Do NOT book tight connections (< 3 hours international)

## When NOT to Use Virtual Interlining

- Traveling with infants or small children (too stressful)
- First-time international traveler (complexity risk)
- Tight schedule with no room for delay
- When savings < $100 person (not worth the risk)
- When transit hub requires a visa you don't have
