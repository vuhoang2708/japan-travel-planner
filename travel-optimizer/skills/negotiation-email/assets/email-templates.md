# Email Templates for Airline Negotiation

## Template 1: Initial Corporate Rate Request (Sales Manager)

**Tone:** Professional, data-driven, confident

```
Subject: [Company] - [Volume] Annual Business Flights - Partnership Discussion

Dear [Corporate Sales Team / Mr./Ms. Name],

I'm [Name], [Title] at [Company]. We're evaluating airline partnerships for
[Year], with approximately [Volume] flights annually on [Route 1] and [Route 2].

[X]% of our [Previous Year] bookings were with [Airline], representing $[Range]
in annual spend. As we finalize our [Year] travel budget, we're reviewing
proposals from several carriers to ensure competitive rates.

We're seeking a [10-15]% improvement on published [Economy/Business] fares for
our primary routes, in exchange for a volume commitment of [X flights/month]
over 12 months. We value [specific benefit: direct routes, schedule reliability,
lounge access] and would prefer consolidating bookings with a single carrier.

Could we schedule a call before [date, 2-3 weeks out]? We're finalizing travel
arrangements for [next quarter].

Best regards,
[Name]
[Title], [Company]
[Phone] | [Email]

CC: [Finance Director / CEO]
```

## Template 2: Reservations Team (Smaller Volume)

**Tone:** Polite, relationship-focused, appreciative

```
Subject: Corporate Rate Inquiry - [Company] ([Volume] flights/year)

Dear [Airline] Corporate Reservations,

My name is [Name] and I manage travel for [Company]. We fly [Volume] trips
annually, primarily [Route 1], and have been loyal [Airline] passengers.

We're exploring whether corporate or negotiated rates might be available for
our travel volume. Our team values [Airline]'s [service/schedule/network], and
we'd like to deepen our partnership.

Would it be possible to discuss corporate fare options? Even a modest
improvement would help us justify consolidating more of our bookings with
[Airline] rather than spreading across multiple carriers.

I'm available at your convenience. Our travel planning for [quarter] closes
[date].

Thank you for your time,
[Name]
[Title], [Company]
[Phone]
```

## Template 3: Key Account Renewal

**Tone:** Collaborative, loyalty-aware, strategic

```
Subject: [Company] Account Review - [Year] Partnership Renewal

Dear [Account Manager Name],

As we approach our [Year] renewal, I'd like to discuss optimizing our
partnership. Over the past 12 months:

- Total bookings: [Volume] flights
- Primary routes: [Route 1], [Route 2], [Route 3]
- [Airline] share of our travel: [X]%
- Total spend: $[Range]

We've appreciated [specific benefit]. For [Year], we're prepared to increase
our commitment to [higher volume/percentage] if we can align on competitive
pricing that reflects our loyalty and volume.

The market has shifted since our last agreement - we've received approaches
from [competitor category, not name], and want to ensure our partnership
remains mutually beneficial.

Can we meet [in person / virtually] before [date] to review terms?

Best regards,
[Name]
```

## Template 4: Follow-Up (No Response After 5 Days)

```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject] - Following Up

Dear [Name],

Following up on my email from [date]. We're in the final stages of selecting
our airline partner for [Year] and would value the opportunity to discuss
terms with [Airline].

Our decision timeline is [date]. I'd appreciate even a brief response on
whether corporate rates are available for our volume.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

## Template 5: Counter-Offer Response

```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject] - Counter-Proposal

Dear [Name],

Thank you for the [X]% proposal. While we appreciate the offer, our analysis
of current market rates for [Route] suggests there's room for improvement.

We've benchmarked against published fares and competitive alternatives:
- [Route 1]: Current market leaders offer [range description]
- Our volume commitment of [X flights/month] represents significant guaranteed revenue

We'd be comfortable proceeding with [target %, higher than their offer but
lower than your real max] on [specific routes]. This reflects both the value
of our commitment and current market conditions.

Can we find a middle ground that works for both sides?

Best regards,
[Name]
```

## Sending Best Practices

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| Best day | Tuesday - Thursday |
| Best time | 9-11 AM recipient's timezone |
| CC | Finance Director or CEO (shows authority) |
| Format | Plain text preferred over HTML |
| Attachment | None initially (attach data only if requested) |
| Follow-up | 5 business days, max 2 follow-ups |
